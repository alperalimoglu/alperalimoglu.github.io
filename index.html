I am a computer engineering researcher and software developer with expertise in blockchain technology and distributed systems. I obtained my Ph.D. from the Department of Computer Engineering at Boğaziçi University in 2024, supervised by Prof. Can Özturan. In my Ph.D. studies, I focused on developing autonomous blockchain-based computational brokers for e-science applications, creating novel frameworks that ensure trust and transparency in distributed computational processes.

Previously, I worked as a Research Project Assistant at Binghamton University (SUNY) from 2012-2015, where I conducted research in machine learning, developing Echo State Network models for time series prediction and workload forecasting, and hybrid SSD/HDD file system designs.

My research interests are broadly in blockchain technology, distributed systems, and computational brokers—specifically, autonomous workflow execution, smart contract-based organizations, and trust mechanisms in distributed environments. I believe that blockchain-based autonomous systems can provide scalable solutions for managing computational workflows while ensuring transparency and reliability in e-science applications.
